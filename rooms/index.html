Governance and founder safeguards

Collaborative by design, but not unmanaged.

Members can propose, discuss, vote, build, and help shape the ecosystem when secure access exists. Final safeguards remain in place to protect LRC's core values, trust standard, safety rules, ownership model, referral integrity, and long-term mission.

The founder acts as a safeguard for core values, not as a blocker of healthy collaboration. Any action that threatens safety, ownership, trust, privacy, legal boundaries, access standards, or the mission can be paused, revised, or rejected through core-values review.

Invitation

Referral starts review

An invitation can start a conversation. It does not grant room access, role authority, ownership, payment, compensation, or rank.

Auth

Identity before entry

Future rooms need secure sign-in and a clear record of who entered, which role they used, and why access was allowed.

RBAC

Role controls by default

Viewer, reviewer, contributor, builder, steward, counsel, room lead, admin, and founder safeguard permissions must stay separate.

Audit

Actions need records

Room entry, acknowledgement, edits, uploads, decisions, exports, access changes, and removals should be logged.

Tests

Permissions must fail closed

Tests should prove the wrong role cannot read, edit, export, approve, invite, view files, or change access.

Removal

Access can be revoked

Misuse, extraction, unsafe claims, pressure, or disrespect for ownership can end access.

AI safety orientation preview

The first orientation teaches what AI can and cannot do here.

Dynamic rooms should begin with safety orientation, not a blank workspace. People should know the action boundary before prompts, files, customers, tasks, or code enter the room.

Preview only Draft mode Owner approval

Orientation shell

AI can prepare the room. People approve the action.

This orientation will explain local drafting, data limits, approval gates, safe escalation, and what to do when a request touches real-world consequences.

Draft-safe

AI may summarize, outline, classify, check, and prepare review materials inside the approved room scope.

Action-blocked

AI may not spend money, file, publish, contact customers, change permissions, export private data, or bypass access controls.

Lesson 1

AI basics

AI output is a starting point for review. It can help organize work, but it does not approve itself.

Lesson 2

Privacy and confidentiality

Do not enter sensitive records, passwords, private keys, payroll data, legal documents, or personal information unless the approved secure workflow exists.

Lesson 3

Review gates

Payments, publishing, filings, messages, accounts, uploads, deletion, and access changes pause for human review.

Lesson 4

Audit records matter

Important room actions should be traceable so quality, ownership, and misuse can be reviewed.

Safety and disclaimer

Rooms support collaboration and learning; they do not replace professional review.

LRC Rooms is a collaboration and learning space for approved members. It may support discussion, planning, voting, project work, AI-assisted summaries, and internal coordination after the secure layer exists. It does not provide legal, tax, payroll, accounting, HR compliance, employment-law, investment, or professional advice.

Sensitive actions require the proper review gate before anything is filed, paid, published, submitted, sent, or relied on. Phase 1 adds no private dynamic data, internal collaboration tools, client-side AI provider keys, or public room entry.
