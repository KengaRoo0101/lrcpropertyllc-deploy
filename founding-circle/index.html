Purpose

AI, people, our future.

Money can make the work durable, but money is not the center. The purpose is to help capable people integrate AI responsibly, build useful tools, exchange skill and trust more directly, and prepare for a future where human judgment matters more, not less.

Purpose

Money follows useful work.

Revenue should support the mission, compensate people fairly, and fund the safety work required to grow.

People

Human judgment stays central.

AI can draft, organize, check, and connect, but people keep responsibility, consent, and final review.

Exchange

Trust creates a modern barter layer.

Trusted people can trade skill, time, tools, relationships, and care more directly when the rules are clear.

The offer

The work is the sale.

This is not a hype pitch. It is a purpose-led build: useful tools, clear roles, attorney-reviewed agreements, approval layers, referral responsibility, and AI that helps people do serious work without taking control away from them.

Trust

Hand picked, not open-door.

People are invited because their judgment, capability, and character may strengthen the ecosystem.

Integrity

Build without taking.

Creators keep respect for their own work. Shared use, licensing, ownership, or revenue must be written clearly.

Safety

Approval before power.

No one receives customer data, payment access, admin access, deploy rights, or public authority by default.

Fairness

Attorneys before deep work.

Serious contributions need proper agreements before money, data, code ownership, or public representation.

Stewardship

Grow one person at a time.

Each trusted person can eventually refer another, but referrals should carry the same standard and never create automatic entry.

AI

Tools serve people.

AI should help draft, organize, route, check, and support, while humans keep responsibility and review.

Growth standard

Trusted few first, heavy hitters later.

The first circle exists to get the foundation safe, useful, and reviewable. Larger outside talent, investors, institutions, and major builders should be invited only after legal structure, data policy, ownership rules, trial review, and access controls are strong enough to protect the mission.

Governance guardrails

No pyramid, no paid recruiting, no automatic access.

This is not a recruiting chain. No one buys their way in, pays to add someone, earns a rank for adding people, or receives a commission for referrals. Entry should come from trust, observed quality, a trial period, and approval by people responsible for the standard.

Entry

Referral starts review.

A referral can open a conversation, but it does not grant access, authority, ownership, payment rights, or membership.

Trial

Quality is tested first.

New people should start with a scoped trial: review, test, draft, or build one small thing before deeper invitation.

Accountability

Inviters carry responsibility.

If someone misuses access, the person who invited them can be reviewed, paused, or removed for poor stewardship.

Ownership

Work belongs to its creator.

Tasks, systems, tools, notes, knowledge, and work product belong to the person who creates them unless a written agreement says otherwise.

Power

Access stays limited.

AI capability, customer data, payments, deploy rights, and public authority stay gated behind specific approval.

Removal

Privilege can be revoked.

This site demands quality. Misuse, extraction, unsafe claims, pressure, or disrespect for ownership can end access.

Participation layers

Trust earns deeper access.

The Founding Circle should grow slowly. Someone can start as a reviewer, then move toward contributor or builder status only after clear work, clear agreement, and clear boundaries.

Layer 1

Trial viewer

Can see the proof of concept, understand why they were invited, and complete a limited review period.

Access: no private systems, no customer data, no code rights.

Layer 2

Reviewer

Can give feedback on clarity, usefulness, risk, and team member experience.

Access: scoped review only; no public authority.

Layer 3

Contributor

Can help with a defined task, draft, workflow, test, or support process.

Access: written scope and review required.

Layer 4

Builder

Can create or improve a tool/module under written ownership and licensing terms.

Access: limited by project agreement.

Layer 5

Partner path

Can help shape a larger branch after trust, competence, and agreements are proven.

Access: deeper, formal, and still approval-gated.

Referral

Responsible invitation

Can recommend another person only when they understand the values and safety standard.

Access: referral does not create automatic entry or payment.

Creator respect

What they build can be theirs. What joins LRC must be clear.

The goal is not for one person to absorb every idea. The goal is to build a careful umbrella where trusted people can create serious tools, keep their identity, and decide what connects to LRC through written consent.

Theirs

Independent creations

Work they bring or build independently should be respected as theirs unless a written agreement says otherwise.

Shared

Co-created work

Joint work needs a project sheet covering ownership, use, revenue, responsibility, and exit rules.

LRC

Umbrella integration

Anything connected to LRC needs explicit approval, safety review, and written terms before launch.

Team work

Tasks and systems are credited.

Useful work, systems, testing, prompts, notes, and learning should be tracked to the person who contributed them.

No extraction

Access is not permission to copy.

Seeing an idea, workflow, or tool does not give anyone permission to reuse it outside the approved scope.

Written terms

Money follows agreements.

Compensation, licensing, revenue share, or ownership changes need clear written terms before the work depends on them.

Founding Circle clause concept

The standard each person must agree to before deeper work.

By participating, each person agrees to protect the integrity of the ecosystem, respect the ownership of each creator's work, keep private information confidential, avoid unauthorized use of tools or ideas, and follow LRC's approval process before public release, payment activity, customer contact, data access, code deployment, or shared representation.

Each person also agrees that LRC is not a pyramid, recruiting chain, or pay-to-enter system. Referrals may only start review. No payment, commission, rank, ownership, or automatic authority is created by inviting someone. Misuse by an invited person can trigger review of the invited person and the person who referred them.

This page is not legal advice and is not a contract. It is the operating standard to take to attorneys so real agreements can protect everyone fairly.
