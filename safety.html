Safety & ownership

Build with AI, keep control.

LRC tools are designed to help people organize ideas, workflows, feedback, and next steps without giving up control of important decisions or sensitive information.

What belongs to you

You keep the rights you already have in your own ideas, notes, files, business details, and submitted content. Sharing an idea through a public form does not assign that idea to LRC.

What belongs to LRC

LRC keeps the rights to its websites, agents, workflows, templates, code, copy, automation structure, brand, and platform design. Do not copy, resell, scrape, clone, or repackage LRC systems without written permission.

What not to share publicly

Public forms are not a confidentiality agreement. Do not submit trade secrets, unreleased inventions, client records, passwords, full EINs, full government identifiers, banking details, private medical details, or proprietary business material unless there is a written agreement and an approved transfer path.

Levi and agent limits

Levi can guide, draft, summarize, route, and prepare handoffs. Levi should stop before anything is submitted, sent, filed, published, purchased, uploaded, deleted, or changed unless clear approval is given.

Public pages default to Starting Plan: Route. Review handoffs can be prepared for the owner, but Level 4 Execute is disabled on the public site unless explicitly approved later.

No paywall or access bypass

LRC agents should use public, authorized, or approved sources. They should not bypass paywalls, login barriers, browser safety warnings, access controls, or permission limits.

Security posture

The live site can be kept behind a password while LRC reviews launch content. Admin access should remain separate from public pages, and sensitive operational keys should stay out of public code, public forms, and chat prompts.

Transparent activity review

LRC records traffic and tool-use events in the admin system so access, page use, submissions, agent actions, payment routes, and preview-gate events can be reviewed. First-party visitor and session ID cookies may connect repeat activity in the audit. Passwords, raw cookie headers, admin codes, and full request bodies should not be stored in the traffic audit.
